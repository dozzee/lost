The Man Who Turned Crypto Millions into Global Change

An exclusive look into Albert Roberts' journey from early Bitcoin investor to humanitarian icon

Published March 15, 2024

"Money is meaningless unless it creates meaningful change." - Albert Roberts

At 68, Albert Roberts could be spending his crypto fortune on luxury yachts and private islands. Instead, he's donated over $700,000 to charitable causes worldwide, with plans to give away even more.

The Early Days

In 2011, when Bitcoin was still a fringe investment, Roberts, then a retired software engineer, decided to take a chance. "I invested $50,000 of my retirement savings into Bitcoin when it was just $30," he recalls with a gentle smile. "My friends thought I was crazy."

The Turning Point

Albert Roberts, inspired by his own struggles, now works tirelessly to support underprivileged children worldwide.

Albert Roberts was not always the man of means he is today. Born in a small, industrial town, his childhood was marked by scarcity and struggle. His father worked long hours in a factory, barely earning enough to keep food on the table, while his mother took on odd jobs to make ends meet. "There were nights when dinner was just bread and tea," Roberts recalls, his voice tinged with the memory of those days. "My siblings and I learned to be creative with nothing."

Despite the hardship, Roberts’ parents instilled in him a relentless drive for education. "My mother always said, 'Knowledge is the one thing no one can take from you.' But staying in school wasn’t easy. There were times I had to skip classes because we couldn’t afford shoes, or when the roof leaked so badly that our books were ruined." He pauses, a shadow of emotion crossing his face. "I remember sitting under a tree after school, reading borrowed books, dreaming of a life where I could change all that."

In his teenage years, tragedy struck when his father suffered a debilitating injury, leaving the family without their primary source of income. Roberts dropped out of school at 16 to work odd jobs, from cleaning factories to delivering newspapers. "I was angry at the world," he admits. "But my mother’s resilience kept us going. She always said, 'Someday, Albert, you’ll use your struggles to help others.' I didn’t believe her then. I do now."

It wasn’t until his late 20s that Roberts managed to return to school. With the help of a kind teacher who saw his potential, he earned a scholarship to study software engineering. He worked tirelessly, often balancing three part-time jobs alongside his studies. "I never wanted to feel powerless again," he explains. "And I promised myself that if I ever had the means, I’d make sure no child would have to go through what I did."

A Dream Fulfilled

Today, that promise is at the heart of Roberts' philanthropy. Through his donations, he has funded schools in rural areas, provided scholarships for underprivileged children, and delivered critical resources to orphanages worldwide. "When I see a child smiling because they finally have books, or hear that a young girl can now attend school because we funded a new classroom, I feel like I’m giving my younger self a second chance," he says, his voice cracking with emotion.

"We are all shaped by our past. Mine taught me that the greatest wealth isn’t what you accumulate—it’s what you give away to make the world a better place."

Roberts has recently launched a program specifically for children who have experienced homelessness, a cause particularly close to his heart. "I know what it feels like to sleep under a roof that doesn’t feel safe, to wonder where your next meal will come from. These kids don’t need pity. They need opportunities and someone to believe in them."

Through his actions, Albert Roberts has become a symbol of hope for countless children worldwide. "I don’t see them as charity cases," he emphasizes. "I see them as future doctors, artists, engineers—the leaders who will create a brighter tomorrow. They’re my legacy."

That investment would eventually grow to millions, but Roberts' perspective on wealth changed dramatically in 2020. "During the pandemic, I saw so much suffering around the world. Here I was, sitting on this digital fortune, and I asked myself - what's it all for?"

Why Give It All Away?

In a recent interview at his modest Seattle home, Roberts addressed the question many have asked: Why not enjoy his wealth in his golden years?

"I've lived a full life. I've had my adventures. Now, my greatest joy comes from seeing how this digital wealth can transform real lives. When people ask why I don't travel the world instead, I tell them I am - through every child we help educate, through every forest we help preserve, through every life we help save."

Looking Forward

Roberts isn't done yet. He plans to donate at least 90% of his remaining crypto wealth over the next five years. "The beauty of crypto is its potential to democratize finance. But its true value lies in what we choose to do with it."
